Extenstion Field - If \(K\) is a field containing the subfield \(F\), then \(K\) is said to be an extension field of \(F\), denoted \(K/F\).
The degree of a field extension \( K/F \), denoted \( \left[ K : F \right] \), is the dimension of \(K\) as a vector sapce over \(F\). The extension is said to be finite if \( \left[ K : F \right] \) is finite and is said to be infinite otherwise.
